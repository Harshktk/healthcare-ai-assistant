# Insurance Eligibility — Frequently Asked Questions

**Document type:** Patient FAQ
**Audience:** Patients
**Last updated:** 2026-01-15
**Disclaimer:** This is a synthetic FAQ created for an AI assistant prototype.
It does not represent any real insurer or healthcare provider.

## 1. How do I know if my insurance is accepted?

The practice maintains a list of accepted insurance plans on the patient
portal. You can also call the front desk and provide your insurer's name and
the plan name printed on your insurance card. The team will confirm whether
the plan is in-network, out-of-network, or not accepted.

## 2. What happens if my plan is out-of-network?

Care can still be provided, but the patient may be responsible for a larger
share of the bill. The front desk will share an estimate of patient
responsibility before a non-emergency visit. For elective procedures, a
written cost estimate is provided in advance.

## 3. Do I need a referral to see a specialist?

It depends on the plan. Health maintenance organisation plans usually require
a referral from the primary care clinician before specialist visits are
covered. Preferred provider organisation plans typically do not require a
referral but may charge a higher copayment for out-of-network specialists.
Patients are advised to confirm referral rules with their insurer before the
visit.

## 4. What documents should I bring for eligibility verification?

Patients should bring a current insurance card, a government-issued photo ID,
and any referral or pre-authorisation paperwork issued by the insurer. For
dependents, the policyholder's information may also be required.

## 5. Pre-authorisation

Some services, such as advanced imaging, certain surgeries, and some specialty
medications, require pre-authorisation by the insurer before they are
performed. The practice will initiate the pre-authorisation request, but the
final decision rests with the insurer. Pre-authorisation typically takes
three to seven business days; urgent requests are processed faster.

## 6. Self-pay and discounts

Patients without insurance, or those who choose not to use insurance, may
pay directly. A self-pay discount is available for many routine visits when
the bill is settled at the time of service. Payment plans can be arranged
for larger bills; please contact the billing department.

## 7. Coordination of benefits

If a patient is covered by more than one insurance plan, both insurers should
be listed at registration. The practice will submit claims to the primary
insurer first and then to the secondary insurer for any remaining balance.

## 8. What is not covered by insurance?

Coverage rules vary by plan, but the following are commonly not covered:
purely cosmetic procedures, services received outside an emergency from a
non-network provider on plans that require network use, and missed-
appointment fees. Patients are encouraged to call their insurer with the
service code provided by the practice to confirm coverage in advance.
