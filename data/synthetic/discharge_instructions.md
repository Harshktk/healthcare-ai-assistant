# General Discharge Instructions

**Document type:** Patient guidance
**Audience:** Patients being discharged after a routine hospital stay
**Last updated:** 2026-01-15
**Disclaimer:** This is a synthetic patient education document created for an
AI assistant prototype. It is not medical advice. Patients should always
follow the specific written instructions given by their own care team and
contact their clinician if they have questions about their individual
situation.

## 1. Activity and rest

Most patients benefit from gradually returning to their normal activities
over the first one to two weeks after discharge. Light walking is encouraged
several times a day to reduce the risk of blood clots and to support
recovery. Heavy lifting, strenuous exercise, driving while taking sedating
medications, and prolonged travel should be avoided until the care team
clears the patient at follow-up.

## 2. Wound care

If the patient has been discharged with a surgical wound or a dressing, the
care team will provide written wound-care instructions. In general:

- Keep the wound clean and dry for the time period specified in the written
  instructions
- Wash hands before and after touching the wound or changing the dressing
- Watch for redness, swelling, increasing pain, warmth, drainage, or fever,
  which may indicate infection

If any of those signs of infection appear, contact the clinic during business
hours or call the after-hours line.

## 3. Medications

Patients leave with a written medication list. Patients should take all
medications exactly as prescribed and not stop a medication early even if
they feel better, unless instructed by a clinician. If a dose is missed, the
patient should follow the guidance in the discharge paperwork rather than
doubling the next dose. Pharmacy questions should be directed to the
dispensing pharmacy or the clinic.

## 4. Diet and hydration

Unless told otherwise, patients should eat a balanced diet and drink enough
fluids to stay well hydrated, generally at least six to eight glasses of
water a day. Patients with heart, kidney, or liver conditions should follow
the specific fluid and dietary guidance given by their clinician.

## 5. Follow-up appointments

Most patients are asked to attend a follow-up visit within seven to fourteen
days of discharge. This visit can be in person or via telehealth depending
on the reason for admission. Booking instructions are included in the
discharge paperwork.

## 6. When to seek urgent care

Patients should call the clinic or the after-hours line for any of the
following:

- A fever above 38.5 degrees Celsius (101.3 Fahrenheit) that does not respond
  to recommended over-the-counter measures
- Increasing pain that is not controlled by prescribed medications
- New shortness of breath or chest discomfort
- Signs of wound infection
- Inability to keep down food or fluids for more than twenty-four hours

For severe symptoms such as crushing chest pain, severe difficulty breathing,
sudden weakness or numbness on one side of the body, sudden severe headache,
or fainting, patients should call emergency services or go to the nearest
emergency department immediately. They should not wait for a return call
from the clinic.

## 7. Returning to work or school

A clinician will provide guidance on when it is safe to return to work or
school based on the reason for the hospital stay. A return-to-work or
return-to-school letter can be requested through the patient portal.
