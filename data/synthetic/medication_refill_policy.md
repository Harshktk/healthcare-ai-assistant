# Medication Refill Policy

## 1. How to request a refill

Patients can request a refill through any of the following:

- The patient portal under the "Medications" tab
- A phone call to the front desk during business hours
- A request from the patient's preferred pharmacy directly to our office
- A note added during a scheduled telehealth or in-person visit

Refill requests submitted before noon on a business day are typically processed
the same day. Requests submitted after noon, on weekends, or on public
holidays are processed the next business day.

## 2. Routine refills

A clinician will review the medication, the patient's recent visit history,
and the current prescription to determine whether the refill can be approved
without an additional visit. For most stable chronic medications, up to a
ninety-day supply may be authorised, with refills up to one year as
appropriate.

If the patient has not had a clinical follow-up within the past twelve months,
or if monitoring labs (such as for diabetes or thyroid medications) are
overdue, the clinician may request a brief follow-up visit before approving
the refill.

## 3. Controlled substances

Controlled substances cannot be refilled through telehealth and require
either an in-person visit or a video visit conducted under the controlled-
substance exception, in line with applicable regulations. A new written or
electronically transmitted prescription is required for each fill of a
schedule II medication; standing refills are not permitted. Patients on
long-term controlled-substance therapy will typically be asked to attend an
in-person review at least once every six months.

## 4. After-hours and weekend refills

The on-call clinician can authorise a short-term emergency refill, generally
up to a three-day supply, for medications that the patient cannot safely
discontinue, such as anti-seizure medications, blood-pressure medications, or
insulin. After-hours refills cannot be issued for controlled substances or
for medications the patient has not previously been prescribed at this
practice.

## 5. Pharmacy of record

Patients should keep a single primary pharmacy on file. If a refill is needed
at a different pharmacy, for example while travelling, the patient should
contact the front desk and provide the pharmacy name, address, and phone
number. The refill will be transferred subject to clinician review.

## 6. Refill denials

If a refill request is declined, the patient will be notified through the
patient portal or by phone with the reason for the decision and the
recommended next step, which is typically scheduling a follow-up visit or
completing overdue lab work.
