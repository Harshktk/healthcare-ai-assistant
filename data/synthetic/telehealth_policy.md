# Telehealth Consultation Policy

**Document type:** Operational policy
**Audience:** Patients and clinical staff
**Last updated:** 2026-01-15
**Disclaimer:** This is a synthetic policy document created for an AI assistant
prototype. It does not represent any real healthcare provider.

## 1. Eligible visit types

Telehealth consultations are available for the following visit types:

- Routine follow-ups for stable chronic conditions
- Medication review and renewal
- Mental health counselling and therapy sessions
- Lab and imaging result discussion
- Minor symptoms that do not require physical examination, such as common
  cold, mild allergy, or skin rash review

The following visit types must be conducted in person and are not eligible for
telehealth: initial paediatric well-child visits, suspected fractures, chest
pain, severe abdominal pain, pregnancy confirmation visits, and any procedure
that requires hands-on examination.

## 2. Medication refills via telehealth

Patients may request a medication refill during a telehealth visit if the
medication has previously been prescribed by a clinician at this practice and
does not require an in-person evaluation. Controlled substances, including
opioid pain medications, benzodiazepines, and stimulants for ADHD, cannot be
refilled through telehealth and must be reviewed in person.

A refill request submitted through the patient portal will be reviewed by the
prescribing clinician within two business days. Urgent refill requests should
be marked accordingly and will be triaged the same day.

## 3. Technical requirements

Patients need a smartphone, tablet, or computer with a working camera and
microphone, and a stable internet connection. The telehealth platform supports
the latest two versions of Chrome, Safari, Firefox, and Edge browsers.
A test call link is sent in the appointment confirmation email so patients can
verify their setup before the visit.

## 4. Privacy and consent

Patients must provide informed consent before the first telehealth visit. The
session is conducted over an encrypted, HIPAA-compliant video platform.
Sessions are not recorded by default. If a recording is needed for clinical or
quality-assurance reasons, the clinician will obtain explicit verbal consent
on the call before recording starts.

## 5. Limitations and escalation

If during a telehealth visit the clinician determines that an in-person
examination is needed, the visit will be converted to a referral for an
in-person appointment, and the telehealth visit fee will be applied as credit
toward the in-person visit. If the patient develops symptoms that suggest a
medical emergency during the call, the clinician will end the call and advise
the patient to call emergency services or go to the nearest emergency
department immediately.
